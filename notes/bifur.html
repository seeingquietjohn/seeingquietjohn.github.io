Introduction

We provide examples and classification of bifurcations in the study of differentiable dynamical systems.

Linear Systems

As a motivating example, we begin with a review of linear dynamical systems \[\frac{dx}{dt} = Ax.\]

Normal Reproduction. Consider the equation \[\frac{dx}{dt} = ax(t).\] Then for real \(k\), the family of curves \(x(t) = ke^{at}\) is a general solution since \[\frac{dx}{dt} = ake^{at} = ax(t).\] Moreover, given an intial value condition \(x_0 = u_0\), the resulting solution \[x(t) = u_0e^{at}\] is unique (Picard-Lindelof).
