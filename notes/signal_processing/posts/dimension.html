Notion of Dimension for Time Series

Introduction

Notion of dimension (the number of degrees of freedom, in other words, the number of independent components) for 1D signals. (Some may call this complexity...)
Examples discussed include polynomial-like, quasiperiodic, and chaotic time series. "theoretical" notions include vector space dimension, topological dimension, fractal dimension, etc. can be obtained by (non)linear manifold learning (the # of orthogonal components), fourier analysis (the # of independent frequences or modes; the k-torus T^k), temporal embedding (takens' condition), etc. "statistical" or "practical" notions -- taking sampling and noise into account -- include estimates of fractal dimension (correlation dimension, etc.), singular spectrum analysis (the # of independent components that can be reliably estimated from the data), etc.
