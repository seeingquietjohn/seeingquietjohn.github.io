Ergodic Theory of Dynamical Systems

Introduction

We consider a time series \(f(t)\) that is in fact \[f(t) = h(F(t)).\] In other words, \(f(t)\) is an observed time series of some hidden (and possibly much more complicated) time series \(F(t) : M \to N\). For simplicity, we will assume \(f(t)\) is one-dimensional and the observation function \(h\) is some one dimensional "projection" \(h: N \to \mathbb{R}\). Usually the goal here is to "reconstruct" \(F\) from \(f\). We will discuss any relevant ideas, interpretation, and problems that occur in this extremely general setting.

Statistical Properties of Dynamical Systems

Introduction

In this section we work with time series generated by a nonlinear dynamical system \(F:\mathbb{R}^m\to\mathbb{R}^n\), i.e., \[x_t = F_\mu (x_0)\] where \(x_0\) denotes the initial condition and \(\mu\) is some system parameter. Here we discuss the statistical properties asymptotic behavor of \(F_\mu\) as \(\mu\) is varied, which isn't fully captured by the classical (geometric) methods in differential equations or spectral theory for quasiperiodic systems.
