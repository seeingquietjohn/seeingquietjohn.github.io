What is an Attractor?

Introduction

The attractor of a dynamical system \(f: M \to M\) plays an extremely important role in characterizing the long-term behavior of \(f\). Here we introduce the definition and a few examples of (strange) attractors.

Attractor of a Dynamical System

Given a map \(f_\mu: M \to M\), the most direct way to understand its long-term evolution is to simply track its phase space, the set of all possible points in \(M\) visited by the iterates \(f^t\). Informally speaking, an attractor of a dynamical system \(f_\mu: M\to M\) is the set of points in \(M\) to which most points evolve to under successive iterates of \(f\).
