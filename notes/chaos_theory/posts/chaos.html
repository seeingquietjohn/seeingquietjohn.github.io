On the Notion of Chaos

Introduction

Definition and examples of simple chaotic systems characterized by sensitive dependence on initial conditions (per Devaney) for discrete dynamical systems. (Compare Simple Chaos and Toplogical Conjugacy.)

Definition of a Chaotic System

Let \(X, d\) be a metric space and \(f\) a map from \(X\) to itself. We say that \(f\) is chaotic if it satisfies the following conditions.

Denseness of periodic points. The set of periodic points of \(f\) is dense in \(X\).
Transitivity. For any two open nonempty subsets \(U, V\) of \(X\), there exists some integer \(n > 0\) such that \(f^n(U) \cap V \neq \emptyset\).
Sensitive Dependence on Initial Condition. For any \(x\in X\) and \(\epsilon > 0\), there exists some \(\delta > 0\) and \(n > 0\) such that \(d(f^n(x), f^n(y)) > \delta\) for some \(y \in X\) with \(d(x, y) < \epsilon\).
