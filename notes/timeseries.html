Introduction

We consider a time series \(f(t)\) that is in fact \[f(t) = h(F(t)).\] In other words, \(f(t)\) is an observed time series of some hidden (and possibly much more complicated) time series \(F(t) : M \to N\). For simplicity, we will assume \(f(t)\) is one-dimensional and the observation function \(h\) is some one dimensional "projection" \(h: N \to \mathbb{R}\). Usually the goal here is to "reconstruct" \(F\) from \(f\). We will discuss any relevant ideas, interpretation, and problems that occur in this extremely general setting.
To write about: (exmaple) \(F(t)\) is periodic (sampling in fourier transform), \(F(t)\) as a dynamical system and reconstruction problems, time-varying observation function \(h_t\), two or more different observation functions on \(F\) (simplest case being that they are related by a linear transform), orthogonal decomposition of \(f\), \(h\) as a (non)linear filter, information theoretic viewpoint and related reconstruction problems, etc.
