Simple Chaos, Smooth Julia Sets, and Topological Conjugacy

← Back to Complex Dynamics

Introduction

In dynamics, we are often interested in the long-term behavior of a system under iteration. In particular, we are interested in the "chaotic" behavior of a system, which is characterized by sensitive dependence on initial conditions. It is then natural to attempt to describe "simple" dynamical systems whose behaviors are easily well-understood. In other words, we are interested in maps \(f\) whose orbit \(\{f^n(x)\}\) is easily tracked for any input \(x\).

Thus, for a holomorphic map \(f:\widehat{\mathbb{C}}\to\widehat{\mathbb{C}}\), we may say that the dynamics of \(f\) is simple if the dynamical dichotomy of the Riemann sphere into the Fatou set \(F_f\) and the Julia set \(J_f\) is topologically simple. It turns out that most Julia sets are complicated fractal subsets of \(\widehat{\mathbb{C}}\), and there are only a few exceptions (up to automorphism of \(\widehat{\mathbb{C}}\)): the closed interval \([-2, 2]\), the unit circle \(S^1\), or the entire sphere \(\widehat{\mathbb{C}}\). Here we will discuss several examples of maps with smooth Julia sets, introducing topological conjugacy along the way.

Examples of Smooth Julia Sets

We begin with a few "base" examples of smooth Julia sets.

The Unit Circle. Let \(s(z) = z^2\) be the squaring map. Then \(J_s\) is the unit circle \(S^1\) since any neighborhood of any \(z = e^{i\theta} \in S^1\) contains points that either converge to \(0\) or \(\infty\), while \(s^n\) converges to either \(z \mapsto 0\) or \(z\mapsto\infty\) on some neighborhood of any \(z \not\in S^1\) as \(n\to\infty\). (Compare Julia and Fatou Sets.)

The Interval \([-2,2]\). Let \(I = [-2,2]\). Then the quadratic polynomial \(f(z) = z^2+c\) maps \(I\) onto itself, while any point outside of \(I\) gets attracted to the point at infinity. In fact, the Julia set \(J_f\) of \(f\) is equal to \(I\).
