Properties of Polynomial Dynamics

We begin with a few relevant dynamical properties of a polynomial.

Existence of Escape Radius. We see that the forward orbits of a polynomial always escape to \(\infty\) given a sufficiently large \(z\). That is, there exists some \(R > 0\) such that \(|f(z)| > \lambda |z|\) for any \(z\) with \(|z| > R \).
Proof. If \(f(z) = a_dz^d + a_{d-1}z^{d-1} + \dots + a_1z + a_0\) is a polynomial, then \[\lim_{z\to\infty}\frac{|f(z)|}{|z|} &= |z|^{d-1}(|a_d + a_{d-1}z^{-1}+\dots+a_0z^{1-d}|) // &= \infty.\]
