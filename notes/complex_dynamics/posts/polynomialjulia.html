Properties of Polynomial Dynamics

We begin with a few relevant dynamical properties of a polynomial. Throughout the section, we let \(f(z) = a_dz^d + a_{d-1}z^{d-1} + \dots + a_1z + a_0\) be a complex polynomial of degree \(d\geq 2\).

Existence of Escape Radius. We see that the forward orbits of a polynomial always escape to \(\infty\) given a sufficiently large \(z\). That is, with \(f\) as above, there exists some \(R > 0\) such that \(|f(z)| > \lambda |z|\) for some \(\lambda > 1\) for any \(z\) with \(|z| > R \).
Proof. We have \[\lim_{z\to\infty}\frac{|f(z)|}{|z|} = \lim_{z\to\infty} |z|^{d-1}(|a_d + a_{d-1}z^{-1}+\dots+a_0z^{1-d}|) = \infty.\]

What happens dynamically when \(f(z)\) escapes?

Escape Criterion. With \(f\) and \(R\) as above, if \(|z| > R\), then \(|f^n(z)| \to \infty\) as \(n\to\infty\).
Proof. The lower bound on \(|f(z)|\) implies $$ \begin{aligned} |f^n(z)| &> \lambda |f^{n-1}(z)| \\ &> \lambda^n |z|. \end{aligned} $$ Since we assume \(\lambda > 1\), it follows that \(|f^n(z)| \to \infty\) as \(n \to \infty\).

Escape Radius. We can indeed compute the escape radius explicitly. Let \( R = \sup\{1, \frac{2+A}{|a_d|}\}\) where \(A = \sum_{i=0}^{d-1}a_i\). Then for any \(|z| > R\), we have $$ \begin{aligned} |f(z)| &\geq |a_d||z|^d - (|a_{d-1}||z|^{d-1} + \dots + |a_1||z| + |a_0|) \\ &\geq |a_d||z|^d - A|z|^{d-1} \\ &\geq |z|^{d-1}(|a_d||z| - A) \\ &\geq 2|z|. \end{aligned} $$

The Filled Julia Set

The escape phenomena gives rise to another interesting dichotomy of the Riemann sphere \(\widehat{\mathbb{C}}\) for complex polynomials: the filled Julia set \[K_f = \{z \in \widehat{\mathbb{C}} : \text{$f^n(z)$ is bounded}\}\] and its complement attracting basin of infinity \[A_f(\infty) = \{z \in \widehat{\mathbb{C}} : \lim_{n\to\infty}f^n(z) = \infty\}.\] The filled Julia set is extremely useful when studying polynomial dynamics, since it is a compact, invariant set whose boundary \(\partial K_f\) is precisely the Julia set \(J_f\).

Invariance of \(K_f\). The filled Julia set \(K_f\) of a complex polynomial \(f\) is completely invariant. That is, \[f^{-1}(K_f) = K_f = f(K_f).\] Proof. This follows immediately from the fact that \[f^{-1}(z) \in K_f \iff z \in K_f \iff f(z) \in K_f.\]

Compactness of \(K_f\). With \(f\) as above, \(K_f\) is a compact subset of \(\mathbb{C}\).
Proof. We indeed want to show that \(K_f\) is closed and bounded. Since \(f\) has an escape radius \(R\), \[K_f \subset \{z : |z| \leq R\}\] so \(K_f\) is bounded. Moreover, by the invariance property, it follows that \[K_f = \bigcap_{n=1}^\infty f^{-n}(\bar{\mathbb{D}}(0, R)).\] Arbitrary intersection of closed sets is closed, so \(K_f\) is closed.
