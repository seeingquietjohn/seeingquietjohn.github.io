Introduction

Informally speaking, dynamics is the study of patterns evolving under some rule in some space. That is, given some function \( f \), we are interested in the long-term behaviors of its orbits \[ \{f(x), f(f(x)), f(f(f(x))), \dots \} \] for input values \( x \).

Complex dynamics is a mathematical formalization of such phenomena. It is the study of the iterates of analytic maps \( f \) on a complex manifold. Here we briefly introduce some of the main ideas in complex dynamics.

Dynamical Decomposition of the Riemann Sphere

As a simple example, consider \( f(z) = z^2 \) on the Riemann sphere \( \widehat{\mathbb{C}} \). We see that the forward iterates of \( f \) inside the open disk \( \mathbb{D} = \{ z : |z| < 1 \} \) converge locally uniformly to zero. Similarly, any iterate of \( f \) converges locally uniformly to the constant map \(z \mapsto \infty\) on \(\widehat{\mathbb{C}}\setminus\overline{\mathbb{D}} = \{ z : |z| > 1 \}\). On the other hand, any forward orbit of \(f\) on the unit circle \( \partial \mathbb{D} = \{ z : |z| = 1 \} \) is "stuck" on the unit circle and any limit of iterates of \(f\) has a jump discontinuity as we "cross" the unit circle.
